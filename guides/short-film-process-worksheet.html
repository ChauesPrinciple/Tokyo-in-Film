The Process for Creating a Short Film

Or Recreating a Scene — A Practical Student Guide

Section 1: Start With Limitations

Identify what you actually have before designing anything. Reminder: A scene recreation starts with the reality of the space you will stand in. Nothing should be imagined that cannot be filmed.

Section 2: Create a Logline That Fits Those Limits

A logline condenses the essential action of the moment into a single, clear sentence. It identifies who the character is in this beat, what they are trying to do, and what stands in their way.

Write your logline below:

Before moving on, confirm that the logline:

  • Can be staged in the location you identified
  • Expresses emotion through action rather than explanation
  • Defines a conflict that can be shown visually
  • Fits within a single beat rather than a full arc

Section 3: Write Only What The Camera Can Show

This step converts your logline into the specific actions the camera will capture. These actions become the basis for your storyboard.

Checklist before moving on:

  • Can the camera film each action exactly as written
  • Does each action represent a clear change, not a repeated motion
  • Can you imagine where the camera would be for each moment
  • Does the sequence contain a beginning, shift, and end

Section 4: Storyboard to Solve Problems Before Shooting

You will draw your actual storyboard panels on a separate sheet. Here, you define the visual decisions each panel must communicate.

Section 6: Shoot According to the Plan

Use your storyboard and the images from the original scene to guide every decision on location. Your goal is not to copy the moment perfectly, but to match its structure.
