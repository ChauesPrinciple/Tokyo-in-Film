Short Form Video Guide

A universal framework for Commercials, Documentaries, or Narrative shorts. This worksheet guides you through the process of creating effective short-form content.

1. Identify the Subject/Business

Definition

Choose a real subject (business, person, or event) and gather essential information directly from the source.

What to do:

  • Pick a subject willing to let you film
  • Interview someone involved (owner, employee, or subject)
  • Ask three questions:
    • "What single service, product, or idea do you want more people to know about?"
    • "Who is your ideal audience/customer for that?"
    • "What do you want viewers to do after watching the video?"
  • Write down their exact answers. These control your entire video.

2. Write the Hook

Definition

A short, spoken line that instantly makes the viewer think, "This is about me."

Choices:
If they feel frustrated → write a problem hook.
If they want improvement → write a desire hook.
If the process looks interesting → write a curiosity hook.
If the result is visually strong → write an outcome hook.

3. Write the Experience Section

Definition

Explain what the viewer goes through from start to finish.

What to do: Write 3-4 short sentences describing the actual journey. Make every sentence filmable.

Example: "You walk in and we look at what you need. We show you the best option right away. The process is quick. You leave with the problem handled."

4. Write the Offer + Call to Action

Definition

State what the viewer gets and tell them exactly what to do.

5. Turn Script into Shot Plan

Definition

Decide exactly what the viewer sees for each line of the script. Assign a visual that clearly shows that moment.

Examples:
Line: "You walk in." → Shot: Front door opening.
Line: "We fix what's wrong." → Shot: Employee using tool on bike.

6. Shooting Order

Strategy

Film in a sequence that prevents missing anything.

  1. Film the Hook first.
  2. Film the CTA second.
  3. Record the entire script as narration third.
  4. Then film all B-roll in the same order the script is written.

7. Edit the Commercial

  1. Put the narration on the timeline.
  2. Place the hook shot over the first line.
  3. Place the CTA shot over the last line.
  4. Match every narration line to a corresponding visual.
  5. Cut each shot down to the simplest version.

8. Create the Vertical Version

Reframe for phones. Duplicate your edit, change sequence settings to vertical (9:16), and reframe shots so important elements stay centered.

Choices:
Shift upward to show faces.
Shift downward to show tools.
Use closer shots if wide ones crop poorly.

9. Final Check

Definition

Confirm the video works from the audience's perspective.

  • Hook: Instantly describes the viewer's situation?
  • Middle: Experience is clear and easy to follow?
  • Ending: Action is direct and unmistakable?
