Three-Shot Version: Car Wash

SHOT 1 — THE DRIVEWAY / THE TIME-LAPSE / THE MATH

EXT. MODERN HOUSE – DRIVEWAY – DAY
TIME-LAPSE:
The FATHER washes and waxes his classic Mercury Cougar by hand. Sponge, rinse, polish, repeat. Long, careful cycles.
In the same driveway, farther back, the SON quickly cleans his modern compact car with spray wax. Short sprays, fast wipes, no rhythm.
REAL TIME RETURNS:
The father finishes the last section of waxing.
The son walks past.
FATHER
They said on the news this morning that a spray wax like the one you use is being recalled. It takes a year off the paint and makes it peel early.
The son shrugs.
SON
So what
FATHER
Why do you use it then
The son points at the wax haze on the hood.
SON
I can explain.
He places his finger into the wax residue and writes directly into the hood:
8,760 HOURS IN A YEAR
SLEEP: 8 × 365 = 2,920
8,760 – 2,920 = 5,840
WORK: 40 × 52 = 2,080
5,840 – 2,080 = 3,760
SON
...and...
Cleaning: 3 × 52 = 156
The equations fill the hood.
SON
This is everything you get. Three thousand, six hundred four free hours. And one hundred fifty-six of them go right here.
The father stares at the numbers scratched through the wax.
CUT TO:

SHOT 2 — THE SPRAY WAX

EXT. SAME DRIVEWAY – CONTINUOUS
The son taps the “156 HOURS” line.
SON
How much would you pay for that time
The father thinks, and shrugs.
FATHER
Five thousand dollars.
SON
That’s less than the cost of a new paint job. And last Sunday I was helping Mom with the puppies while you were out here.
The father takes the spray wax bottle from the son’s hand.
FATHER
You just spray it on then
SON
Not gonna say anything else.
The father sprays a small patch on the compact car’s hood. Wipes. Looks at his hand.
FATHER
It just rinses off.
SON
Yep.
They both look at the spray bottle.
CUT TO:

SHOT 3 — THE DOOR

EXT. FRONT DOOR – MOMENTS LATER
The father walks toward the house holding the spray wax.
He goes inside.
The door closes.
From inside, off-screen:
VOICE (O.S.)
What are you working on
HOLD.
CUT TO BLACK.
