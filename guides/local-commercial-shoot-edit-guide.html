How to Shoot & Edit Local Business Commercials

Core Goal: You're not making a pretty montage. You're making a 60-second ad that hooks the viewer instantly, shows the experience clearly, and ends with a specific call to action.

Pre-Production

Talk to the Client

Before you touch a camera, clarify:

  • What are we selling (Approx. exact service/offer)
  • Who is it for
  • What should viewers do (Click, book, call, visit)
  • Where will it live (Website 16:9, Social 9:16, or both)

Write a Simple Script

Aim for ~60 seconds. Structure:

  1. Hook (First line): Hit a problem or desire directly. "If you're feeling tense... it's time to reset."
  2. Experience/Solution: Walk them through the steps. Focus on sensory details.
  3. Offer + CTA: specific instruction. "Click below to book."

On-Set Workflow

Order of Operations

  1. Shoot the Hook.
  2. Shoot the Outro / Call to Action.
  3. Record the Entire Script as Audio (voiceover style) while talent is mic'd.
  4. Shoot B-Roll to cover the whole script.
  5. Finish with extra detail shots.

General Shooting Practices

Frame Rate and Motion

  • 24/25 fps: Main narrative, hook, talking heads.
  • 30 or 60 fps: B-roll (for subtle slow motion of hands, water, textures).

Lighting Basics

  • Key Light: Place so subject faces slightly toward it. Soften it to match the mood.
  • Back Light: Separate talent from background.
  • Ambient: Lift exposure enough to be readable, but don't kill the mood (cozy vs bright).

Audio

  • Use lav mics for spoken lines.
  • Monitor levels to avoid clipping.
  • Record room tone.

Directing Performance

The Hook: Direct it like a mini-scene. Action -> Walk in -> Deliver strong line.

The CTA: Get multiple versions (Short/Punchy vs. Descriptive).

The Voiceover: Have them read the full script naturally while mic'd, even if off-camera. This becomes your narration track.

B-Roll Strategy

Match visual shots to the script beats:

  • Arrival: Exterior, door, logo, walking in.
  • Settle In: Reclining, textures (towels, oils), close-ups.
  • Service in Action: Hands working, different angles (OTS, profile, tight).
  • Afterglow/Exit: Relaxed client, smiling, leaving.
Shot Variety: For each moment, get a Wide, Medium, and Close-up. You need options to keep the edit pacing fast (2-3 seconds per shot).

Editing the Commercial

Workflow

  1. Build Structure: Drop voiceover script into timeline. Place Hook video at start.
  2. Cover with B-Roll: Lay visuals over the narration chronologically.
  3. Pacing: Aim for 2-3 seconds per shot. Cut early to keep energy up.

Vertical Delivery (9:16)

  • Edit horizontal first to lock the story.
  • Duplicate timeline to vertical sequence.
  • Reframe each shot: slide up/down to keep faces/action centered.
  • Captions: Essential for vertical. Use bold, readable fonts. Add a gradient bar behind text if needed.

Color and Finishing

  • Conversion: Apply Log-to-Rec.709 if needed.
  • Correction: Fix exposure and white balance. Check skin tones.
  • Style: Add subtle creative grade (warmth for spa, contrast for gym). Don't overdo it.
