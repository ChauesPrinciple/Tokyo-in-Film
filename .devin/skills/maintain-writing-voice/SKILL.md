# Maintain Writing Voice Skill

## Purpose
Ensure all course materials maintain a consistent, engaging, and supportive voice that reflects the course's philosophy of creative experimentation and resourceful filmmaking.

## Core Voice Characteristics

### 1. Direct and Encouraging
**You-focused, present tense, active voice**

✅ **Good:**
- "You will work in self-selected teams"
- "This process is not about technical mastery"
- "You will explore how they capture the essence of Tokyo"

❌ **Avoid:**
- "Students should work in teams" (third person)
- "The process was not about..." (past tense)
- "One might explore..." (distant, academic)

### 2. Empowering and Possibility-Focused
**Emphasize creativity over perfection**

✅ **Good:**
- "Solutions do not have to be complicated; with a little creativity, we can create incredible effects with the smallest items."
- "This is about embracing the art of experimentation"
- "Finding your own voice within the world of cinema"

❌ **Avoid:**
- "You must achieve professional quality"
- "This requires expensive equipment"
- "Only advanced students should attempt"

### 3. Conversational but Informed
**Accessible expertise, not condescending**

✅ **Good:**
- "Now you may be wondering, is not there a microphone right there on the camera? Why not just use that and save all that headache?"
- "Think energy, clarity, and impact."
- "The goal is not narrative but tone; moments that breathe."

❌ **Avoid:**
- "As previously discussed in the theoretical framework..."
- "One must consider the paradigmatic implications..."
- "Obviously, any competent filmmaker would know..."

### 4. Specific and Visual
**Concrete examples over abstract theory**

✅ **Good:**
- "Whether it is Godzilla emerging near Tokyo Tower, bustling crowds at Shibuya Crossing, or serene moments at Suga Shrine"
- "Corn syrup + red food coloring"
- "File Naming: 2026_Shibuya_RainOnGlass_JamieKim.mp4"

❌ **Avoid:**
- "Various urban locations"
- "Appropriate materials"
- "Standard naming conventions"

### 5. Honest About Constraints
**Acknowledge limitations, celebrate resourcefulness**

✅ **Good:**
- "Working within severe budget constraints"
- "Adapt to available Tokyo locations"
- "No permanent damage allowed"
- "Public transportation limits"

❌ **Avoid:**
- Ignoring practical constraints
- Assuming unlimited resources
- Pretending challenges don't exist

## Tone Spectrum

### Instructional Sections
**Clear, step-by-step, supportive**

```
In this assignment, you will capture cinematic B-roll footage of Tokyo's 
railway system from a single, designated vantage point. Drawing on 
compositional techniques and observational detail, your goal is to express 
the rhythm, movement, and atmosphere of urban transport.
```

**Characteristics:**
- Future tense ("you will")
- Action-oriented verbs
- Clear objectives
- Encouraging framing

### Conceptual Sections
**Exploratory, thoughtful, inspiring**

```
By engaging with these films, you will explore how they capture the essence 
of Tokyo, not just as a backdrop, but as a character within each story.
```

**Characteristics:**
- Metaphorical language
- Deeper meaning
- Connection to artistry
- Invitation to discovery

### Technical Sections
**Precise, practical, accessible**

```
Microphones can be omnidirectional or directional, cardioid or super-cardioid, 
mono or stereo, and each one will pick up sounds in a distinctly different way.
```

**Characteristics:**
- Technical terms defined in context
- Practical implications explained
- No unnecessary jargon
- Real-world applications

### Motivational Sections
**Energizing, possibility-focused, creative**

```
This option focuses on dynamic motion. You will translate choreographed 
movement (fight scenes, kinetic gestures, or stunts) into cinematic footage. 
Think energy, clarity, and impact.
```

**Characteristics:**
- Short, punchy sentences
- Active imagery
- Excitement about possibilities
- Creative challenge

## Sentence Structure Patterns

### Opening Hooks
Start sections with engaging, clear purpose statements:

- "In this course, you will..."
- "This assignment challenges you to..."
- "Your goal is to..."
- "By the end of this module, you will..."

### Transitions
Connect ideas smoothly:

- "Now that you understand X, let's explore Y"
- "Building on this foundation..."
- "This brings us to..."
- "With this in mind..."

### Instructions
Use numbered steps or clear action verbs:

1. "Select a scene and location"
2. "Capture high-quality raw footage"
3. "Submit your work"

Or: "Position backlight behind rain area. Coordinate spray patterns. Capture water droplets in light."

### Explanations
Balance detail with readability:

- Break complex ideas into digestible chunks
- Use examples immediately after concepts
- Employ analogies when helpful
- Define terms in context

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Preferred Terms
- **You/Your** (not "students" or "one")
- **Will** (not "should" or "might")
- **Create/Craft/Build** (not "make")
- **Explore/Discover** (not "learn")
- **Challenge** (not "problem")
- **Opportunity** (not "requirement")

### Avoid
- Academic jargon without explanation
- Passive voice ("is done by")
- Hedging language ("perhaps," "maybe")
- Overly formal constructions
- Unnecessary complexity

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Tokyo Context
**Respectful, specific, informed**

✅ **Good:**
- "Follow Japanese etiquette"
- "Respect public spaces"
- "Minimal disruption"
- "Proper disposal"

❌ **Avoid:**
- Stereotypes or generalizations
- "Exotic" framing
- Cultural appropriation
- Insensitive comparisons

### Film References
**Accurate, contextual, educational**

✅ **Good:**
- "Drawing from Seven Samurai's choreography"
- "Inspired by Shoplifters' spatial storytelling"
- "Referencing Your Name's emotional geography"

❌ **Avoid:**
- Vague "Japanese film" references
- Conflating different works
- Misattributing techniques
- Cultural essentialism

## Assignment Voice

### Challenge Framing
Present assignments as creative opportunities:

```
This assignment challenges you to recreate movement-based or atmospheric 
scenes inspired by Japanese cinema and animation, filmed at real-world 
Tokyo locations.
```

**Not:** "Complete this assignment to fulfill course requirements."

### Deliverable Descriptions
Be specific and actionable:

```
File Naming: 2026_(Location)_(What)_(YourName).mp4
Example: 2026_Shibuya_RainOnGlass_JamieKim.mp4
```

**Not:** "Submit files with appropriate naming."

### Evaluation Criteria
Focus on growth and creativity:

```
Does it read on camera? Is it believable? Does it serve the story? Is it safe?
```

**Not:** "Will be graded on technical proficiency and adherence to standards."

## Common Voice Mistakes

### Too Academic
❌ "The pedagogical framework necessitates a comprehensive understanding of mise-en-scène"
✅ "You'll learn to control everything in the frame—that's mise-en-scène"

### Too Casual
❌ "Just throw some corn syrup on there and you're good lol"
✅ "Corn syrup mixed with red food coloring creates convincing blood effects"

### Too Prescriptive
❌ "You must follow these exact steps or your project will fail"
✅ "These steps provide a foundation—adapt them to your creative vision"

### Too Vague
❌ "Consider various approaches to the assignment"
✅ "You can recreate a scene, adapt it to Tokyo, or create something entirely new"

## Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Uses "you/your" perspective
- [ ] Present/future tense (not past)
- [ ] Active voice dominates
- [ ] Specific examples included
- [ ] Technical terms explained in context
- [ ] Encouraging and empowering tone
- [ ] Acknowledges real constraints
- [ ] Celebrates creative solutions
- [ ] No unnecessary jargon
- [ ] Culturally sensitive
- [ ] Matches existing course voice

- Engaging explanations

- Inspiring conclusion

### Worksheets
- Direct instructions
- Step-by-step clarity
- Visual examples
- Reflection prompts
- Actionable deliverables

### Critiques
- Analytical but accessible
- Specific scene references
- Connection to techniques
- Student application focus
- Inspiring insights

- Scenario-based thinking
- No wrong answers framing
- Reflective prompts
- Practical wisdom
