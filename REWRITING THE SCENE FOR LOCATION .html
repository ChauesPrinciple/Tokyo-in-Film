REWRITING THE SCENE FOR LOCATION

1. FRAME-FIRST REWRITE

– What fills the foreground?
– What lines lead the eye? (stairs, alleys, rails, shop lines, horizon)
– What shapes the background? (depth, skyline, crowds)
– What your lens must include or cannot avoid.

2. PHYSICAL LIMITS REWRITE

List the physical limits of the space:
Be specific—limits produce the rewrite.
– Width narrow enough that two people can’t pass
– Crowds that force staggered timing
– Ceiling height that kills an overhead shot
– No place to hide camera, no long push-in path
– Obstacles that break the original choreography
→ Click or tap here to enter text.

Rewrite the scene so every action fits what is physically possible here:
You are not “adjusting” the scene—you are rebuilding it with new rules.
– Replace any action requiring space you don’t have
– Shorten or lengthen motion based on real distance
– Reassign character placement if original positions don’t fit
– Change entrances/exits based on the real physical pathways

3. MOVEMENT REWRITE

Where characters can realistically enter, stand, move, or sit:
List actual workable paths, not ideal ones.
– Natural arrival points the architecture creates
– Where bodies naturally cluster or separate
– Places movement bottlenecks (stairs, corners, chokepoints)
– Where standing looks intentional vs awkward

Identify every action the location rejects:

– Moments requiring privacy in a crowded area
– Any gesture that loses meaning because angle/space won’t support it

– A “walk and talk” becomes a “pause and pivot”
