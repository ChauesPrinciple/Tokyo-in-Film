# OPTICS & PSYCHOLOGY

## Focal Length Fundamentals

Focal length = distance from the lens's optical center to the CCD image plane, expressed in millimeters. For a 1/3" CCD sensor (standard consumer/prosumer video camera), the **normal lens** is approximately **6.5mm**. Normal = what the human eye perceives as natural perspective.

| Lens Type | Focal Length (1/3" CCD) | Perspective Effect | Psychological Register |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Fish eye | ~6mm or less | Extreme barrel distortion, maximum exaggeration of depth | Unreality, madness, nightmare |
| Wide angle | < 6.5mm | Exaggerates depth, distorts edges, places subject in environment | Context, vulnerability, exposure, isolation within space |
| Normal | ~6.5mm | Natural perspective, no distortion | Neutral, observational, documentary reality |
| Telephoto | > 6.5mm | Compresses depth, flattens planes, isolates subject from background | Surveillance, pressure, alien observation, subject-as-object |

**Critical insight**: Wide lenses feel participatory — the camera is IN the space. Telephoto lenses feel observational — the camera watches from distance. A subject shot telephoto feels watched, not accompanied.

## Depth of Field

Depth of field (DOF) = the range of distances that appear acceptably sharp in the image.

**Deep DOF** (wide lens, high f-stop, subject far from camera): Both subject and background are sharp. Subject exists within environment. Context is legible. Audience can read space.

**Shallow DOF** (long lens, low f-stop, subject close to camera): Subject sharp, background blurred (bokeh). Subject isolated from environment. Context is denied. Audience is trapped with the face.

### DOF as Narrative Tool

**Deep DOF in horror**: Used in Stage One to establish the ship as a comprehensible, navigable space. The audience should know where things are before they stop being where they are.

**Shallow DOF in horror**: Used when environment has become unsafe or meaningless. If the background can kill you, blurring it is false safety — but the visual grammar of shallow DOF connotes isolation and intimacy, which becomes horrifying when the intimate presence is the wrong one.

## Focal Length × Shot Size Matrix

The same "close-up" shot means different things shot with different lenses:

| Shot | Wide Lens | Normal Lens | Telephoto Lens |
|------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| MS | Distorted, subject fills space oddly, environment exaggerated | Standard, professional, naturalistic | Compressed, face flattened, subject-as-subject without context |
| CU | Face distorted, exaggerated proximity, almost threatening | Naturalistic intimacy | Face flattened but intensely observed, surveillance quality |
| ECU | Maximum distortion, surreal | Clean, standard horror ECU | Distant observation of a detail — the eye is watched, not watched |

## Lens Distortion as Narrative Tool

**Wide lens distortion (barrel distortion)**: The edges of the frame bow outward. This is subtle at moderate wide angles but becomes grotesque at fish-eye. Used to signal: the world is no longer shaped correctly. For ON THE EDGE, any shot that includes the viewport showing the Entity's approach should use a slightly wider lens than the scene's standard — the universe is bending.

**Telephoto compression**: Objects at different distances appear nearly the same size. The Entity in the far distance appears the same size as a character in the foreground. This is the correct lens for showing incomprehensible scale — the thing that should be infinitely distant looks present.

## Subject-Environment Relationship

Every shot makes a claim about the relationship between subject and environment.

**Wide lens + deep DOF**: Subject is embedded in environment. Environment has power over subject. Used in early ON THE EDGE to show the crew as inhabitants of the ship — the ship contains them.

**Telephoto + shallow DOF**: Subject is extracted from environment. Subject exists alone. Environment is denied. Used in late ON THE EDGE when characters have lost their world.

**Wide lens + ECU**: Subject fills and distorts the frame. The subject has BECOME the environment. Used for psychological disintegration moments — when a character's internal state overwhelms all external reality.

## ON THE EDGE Lens Protocol

| Element | Lens Choice | DOF | Reason |
|---------|-------------|-----|--------|
| Ship interiors, Stage One | Normal | Deep | The ship is knowable, safe, coherent |
| Crew conversations, Stage One | Normal | Medium | Professional distance, legible space |
| Instrument readings, anomaly | Slight wide | Deep | The data exists in the world, not yet internalized |
| Character CU, Stage Two | Telephoto | Shallow | Observed, isolated, pressure |
| Viewport / Entity exterior | Telephoto | Variable | The Entity is always at a distance that shouldn't matter |
| ECU moments, Stage Three | Normal | Shallow | Clean horror — no distortion needed when psychology is already broken |
| Entity (any shot) | Telephoto maximum | N/A — background | The Entity is observed, never intimate, never here |
