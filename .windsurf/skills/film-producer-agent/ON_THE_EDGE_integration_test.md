## INPUT

**Scene from screenplay:**

```
INT. OBSERVATION DECK — TWELVE MINUTES

Reyes alone. Her theoretical models projected on the curved wall.

She pulls up the signal data. Overlays it on her models.

The signal data maps to her equations. She leans in.

Not similar. Identical. Not to her published work — to files she
hasn't opened in months. The incomplete framework. The bridge she
never found.

The bridge is in the signal.

REYES
(staring at the overlay)
When did I —

She searches her files. Her original models: present, incomplete.
The completed version: only here, in the signal.

She opens her files. Works backward.

REYES (CONT'D)
(to herself)
If I solved it, I'd remember solving it. I remember everything I've
solved. I remember —

She pulls up a specific equation. Stares at it.

REYES (CONT'D)
When did I write this.

She writes "When did I write this?" in her margin. Underlines it.
Works.
```

**Stage:** Stage Two: The Amplification
**Character State:** Stable (entering) → Pressured (recognition) → Breaking (confrontation with loss)
**Emotional Tone:** Intellectual dread

---

## STEP 1: @visual-grammar — Shot Size Progression

**Analysis:**
Reyes is alone. The scene is entirely internal — she is discovering something is wrong with her own memory. No interpersonal tension. The horror is cognitive.

Shot size must track her psychological descent:
- She enters the scene as a scientist (stable, functional) → MS
- Recognition that the signal contains her work → CU (tightening)
- Confirmation she didn't write it → CU narrowing
- "When did I write this" — the question that proves the loss → ECU hands on keyboard
- She writes the question in her margin and keeps working → Return to MS (her mechanism: work instead of panic)

**Compositional Notes:**
- Lead room: Initially generous (she has space, she is confident) → progressively reduced as scene escalates
- Headroom: Standard → reduced slightly on CU series (pressure builds above her)
- Rule of thirds: Models on curved wall use full frame — Reyes in lower third, dwarfed by her own work
- The completed equations she didn't write should be centered, symmetrical — confrontational composition

---

## STEP 2: @optics-psychology — Focal Length Selection

**Analysis:**
She is isolated in a room with her work. The space should feel like a container that is slowly closing.

- Opening: Normal lens (10mm equivalent) — she is in her world, it belongs to her
- As the overlay maps perfectly: Shift to telephoto (16mm+) — compression, her face isolated from the equations, the wall behind her goes soft while she goes sharp. She is separated from the thing that used to be hers.
- ECU hands: Normal (10mm, close) — the hands that wrote the equations, now searching for what they wrote
- Final MS: Normal — she returns to function but the telephoto has shown us what's wrong

**DOF Strategy:**
- Opening: Deep DOF — everything visible, she owns this space
- CU series: Shallow DOF — equations blur behind her, she is isolated from her own work

## STEP 4: @acoustic-design — Sound Plan

**Environment:** Observation deck — likely dampened, small, curved walls creating a subtle reverb

**Sound Plan:**
- Primary Mic: Shotgun from above (she is relatively still, turning to console)
- Backup: Lav hidden in collar (for ECU moments when boom can't reach without shadow)
- Boom Placement: Above frame for all MS/CU; switch to below frame for final ECU hands
- Room Tone: 60 seconds of observation deck tone — the hum of projectors, the almost-silence of deep space

**Editorial Audio Opportunities:**
- **J-Cut into this scene:** We hear the signal data playing — barely audible, just below threshold — before we cut to Reyes in the room. The sound is already in her space before we arrive.
- **The overlay moment:** Sound design — when she maps signal to equations, a soft resonance. Not music. The signal recognizing itself.
- **"When did I write this":** Delivered in almost no voice. The boom hears it. The audience has to lean in.
- **L-Cut out of scene:** Her keyboard typing continues into the next scene's opening image. She is still working. The world has changed; she is still working.

## STEP 6: @editorial-grammar — Cut Sequence

**Emotional Arc:** Stable → Recognition → Confrontation → Documentation → Return to function (worse)

```

2. MS (T2) → CU wall equations (T8): Straight cut, 5 sec → 4 sec
   Kuleshov: Her neutral face + equations = curiosity
   The signal data appears in the equations.

3. CU equations (T8) → CU Reyes leans in (T3): Straight cut, 4 sec → 3 sec
   Kuleshov: Equations perfectly mapped + Reyes's face = recognition/dread
   She leans in. Something is wrong in the right direction.

4. CU Reyes (T3) → ECU incomplete model (T9): Straight cut, 3 sec → 3 sec
   Function: Her face of recognition + her incomplete work = she knows what's missing
   
5. ECU incomplete (T9) → CU equations (T8): Jump cut, 3 sec → 3 sec
   The gap is filled. Same frame position. Jump cut = rupture.
   The bridge is there. It shouldn't be.

   "If I solved it, I'd remember solving it."
   Kuleshov: Completed equations she didn't write + her face = the beginning of terror

7. CU Reyes (T4) → ECU hands (T5): Straight cut, 6 sec → 5 sec
   She searches her own files with her own hands.
   The hands are looking for what they did.

8. ECU hands (T5) → ECU margin (T6): Straight cut, 5 sec → 8 sec
   She writes the question. Long take. The writing is slow, deliberate.
   The pen is hers. The handwriting is hers. The question is the proof.

9. ECU margin (T6) → CU Reyes face (T7): Straight cut, 8 sec → 10 sec
   Long take. She keeps working.
   This is the horror: she keeps working.

- Average Shot Length: 5 seconds
- Rhythm: Decelerating then stopping (starts tight, fast recognition; 
  ends in long static takes — the stillness of someone who has decided 
  not to collapse)
- Long Takes: Shots 8 and 9 (8 and 10 sec) — the documentation needs weight
- Rapid Cutting: Shots 4 and 5 (3 sec each, jump cut) — the bridge appearing

TRANSITION TO NEXT SCENE (Marshall's Quarters):

- Reyes's keyboard continues under the cut
```
