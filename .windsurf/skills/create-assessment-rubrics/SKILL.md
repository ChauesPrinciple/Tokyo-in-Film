# Create Assessment Rubrics Skill

## Purpose
Design clear, fair, and pedagogically sound assessment rubrics for student filmmaking projects that provide actionable feedback and encourage creative growth.

## Assessment Philosophy

### Student-Centered Evaluation
- **Growth-focused**: Measure improvement, not just final product
- **Process-valued**: Credit planning, rehearsal, and iteration
- **Specific feedback**: Actionable suggestions, not vague comments
- **Encouraging**: Highlight strengths while addressing weaknesses
- **Fair**: Clear criteria known in advance

### Balancing Technical and Creative
- Technical competence (baseline standards)
- Creative choices (artistic decisions)
- Storytelling effectiveness (narrative impact)
- Problem-solving (resourcefulness)
- Professional workflow (organization and process)

## Rubric Structure

### Standard Rubric Format

**Four Performance Levels**
1. **Exemplary (A)**: Exceeds expectations, professional quality
2. **Proficient (B)**: Meets all expectations, solid work
3. **Developing (C)**: Meets most expectations, needs improvement
4. **Beginning (D/F)**: Does not meet expectations, significant issues

**Five Assessment Categories**
1. **Technical Execution** (20-30%)
2. **Creative/Artistic Choices** (20-30%)
3. **Storytelling/Content** (20-30%)
4. **Process/Workflow** (10-20%)
5. **Presentation/Professionalism** (10-20%)

### Category Weighting Guidelines

**Pre-Production Assignments**
- Planning/Organization: 30%
- Research/Preparation: 25%
- Creative Concept: 25%
- Documentation: 20%

**Production Assignments**
- Technical Execution: 30%
- Creative Choices: 25%
- Problem-Solving: 20%
- Safety/Professionalism: 25%

**Post-Production Assignments**
- Technical Quality: 25%
- Creative Editing: 30%
- Storytelling: 30%
- Polish/Presentation: 15%

**Final Projects**
- Technical Competence: 25%
- Creative Vision: 25%
- Storytelling: 25%
- Process Documentation: 15%
- Presentation: 10%

## Assignment-Specific Rubrics

### Film Critique/Analysis Rubric

**Analysis Depth (30%)**
- Exemplary: Insightful analysis with specific examples, connects techniques to meaning
- Proficient: Clear analysis with examples, understands techniques
- Developing: Basic analysis, some examples, surface-level understanding
- Beginning: Minimal analysis, few examples, misunderstands techniques

**Technical Terminology (20%)**
- Exemplary: Precise use of filmmaking vocabulary, demonstrates mastery
- Proficient: Correct use of terminology, demonstrates understanding
- Developing: Some correct terminology, occasional errors
- Beginning: Incorrect or missing terminology

**Writing Quality (20%)**
- Exemplary: Clear, engaging, well-organized, error-free
- Proficient: Clear, organized, minimal errors
- Developing: Somewhat clear, some organization issues, several errors
- Beginning: Unclear, disorganized, many errors

**Film References (15%)**
- Exemplary: Specific timestamps, frame-by-frame analysis, visual examples
- Proficient: Specific scenes referenced, clear descriptions
- Developing: General references, vague descriptions
- Beginning: No specific references, cannot locate examples

**Cultural Context (15%)**
- Exemplary: Deep understanding of Japanese film context, nuanced analysis
- Proficient: Good understanding of context, makes connections
- Developing: Basic understanding, surface connections
- Beginning: Missing or incorrect cultural context

### Cinematography Assignment Rubric

**Composition (25%)**
- Exemplary: Intentional, sophisticated framing; rule of thirds mastered; creative depth
- Proficient: Good framing, rule of thirds applied, adequate depth
- Developing: Basic framing, some composition issues, flat depth
- Beginning: Poor framing, ignores composition principles

**Lighting (25%)**
- Exemplary: Creative use of available light, mood established, consistent
- Proficient: Appropriate lighting, adequate exposure, mostly consistent
- Developing: Acceptable lighting, exposure issues, inconsistent
- Beginning: Poor lighting, major exposure problems

**Camera Movement (20%)**
- Exemplary: Motivated, smooth, enhances story, professional execution
- Proficient: Appropriate movement, mostly smooth, serves story
- Developing: Some unmotivated movement, stability issues
- Beginning: Shaky, unmotivated, distracts from story

**Coverage (15%)**
- Exemplary: Excellent variety, multiple angles, creative options for editing
- Proficient: Good variety, adequate angles, sufficient for editing
- Developing: Limited variety, missing some angles
- Beginning: Insufficient coverage, cannot edit coherently

**Technical Quality (15%)**
- Exemplary: Sharp focus, proper exposure, clean footage
- Proficient: Acceptable focus and exposure, usable footage
- Developing: Focus/exposure issues, barely usable
- Beginning: Major technical problems, unusable footage

### Action Choreography Rubric

**Safety (Pass/Fail - Must Pass)**
- Pass: No contact, adequate rehearsal, safety protocols followed, no injuries
- Fail: Any actual contact, insufficient rehearsal, safety violations, injuries

**Choreography Design (30%)**
- Exemplary: Creative, varied action units, clear progression, exciting
- Proficient: Solid choreography, adequate variety, logical flow
- Developing: Basic choreography, repetitive, unclear progression
- Beginning: Confusing, poorly designed, no clear structure

**Camera Coverage (25%)**
- Exemplary: Multiple angles, clear action, creative shots, excellent continuity
- Proficient: Good angles, action visible, adequate continuity
- Developing: Limited angles, some unclear action, continuity issues
- Beginning: Poor coverage, action unclear, major continuity problems

**Editing/Impact (25%)**
- Exemplary: Sells impact through editing, excellent rhythm, exciting
- Proficient: Good impact, adequate rhythm, engaging
- Developing: Weak impact, rhythm issues, somewhat boring
- Beginning: No impact, poor rhythm, fails to engage

**Rehearsal Documentation (20%)**
- Exemplary: Extensive documentation, multiple rehearsals, clear progression
- Proficient: Good documentation, adequate rehearsals
- Developing: Minimal documentation, insufficient rehearsals
- Beginning: No documentation, clearly unrehearsed

### Sound Design Rubric

**Dialogue Clarity (25%)**
- Exemplary: Crystal clear, perfect levels, professional quality
- Proficient: Clear and understandable, good levels
- Developing: Mostly understandable, level issues
- Beginning: Unclear, major level problems

**Sound Design/Foley (25%)**
- Exemplary: Rich, detailed, creative, enhances story
- Proficient: Adequate effects, supports story
- Developing: Basic effects, some missing elements
- Beginning: Minimal or poor quality effects

**Music Integration (20%)**
- Exemplary: Perfect music choice, excellent levels, emotional impact
- Proficient: Good music choice, appropriate levels
- Developing: Acceptable music, level issues
- Beginning: Poor music choice or overpowering

**Mix Quality (20%)**
- Exemplary: Professional balance, spatial awareness, polished
- Proficient: Good balance, adequate mix
- Developing: Unbalanced, mix issues
- Beginning: Poor mix, major problems

**Technical Quality (10%)**
- Exemplary: No noise, perfect sync, clean audio
- Proficient: Minimal noise, good sync
- Developing: Some noise, sync issues
- Beginning: Major noise, sync problems

### Editing Assignment Rubric

**Pacing/Rhythm (30%)**
- Exemplary: Excellent rhythm, intentional pacing, builds and releases tension
- Proficient: Good pacing, adequate rhythm, serves story
- Developing: Uneven pacing, rhythm issues
- Beginning: Poor pacing, no sense of rhythm

**Continuity (25%)**
- Exemplary: Flawless continuity, seamless cuts, professional
- Proficient: Good continuity, minor issues
- Developing: Continuity errors, noticeable problems
- Beginning: Major continuity problems

**Storytelling (25%)**
- Exemplary: Clear, engaging, emotional impact, creative choices
- Proficient: Clear story, adequate engagement
- Developing: Story somewhat unclear, limited engagement
- Beginning: Confusing story, fails to engage

**Technical Execution (20%)**
- Exemplary: Clean cuts, perfect sync, smooth playback
- Proficient: Good cuts, adequate sync
- Developing: Rough cuts, sync issues
- Beginning: Major technical problems

## Feedback Framework

### Constructive Feedback Principles

**Sandwich Method**
1. Start with specific strength
2. Address area for improvement
3. End with encouragement

**Be Specific**
- Bad: "Lighting is poor"
- Good: "The key light is too harsh at 0:45, creating unflattering shadows. Try diffusing with white fabric."

**Focus on Craft, Not Taste**
- Bad: "I don't like this music choice"
- Good: "The music overpowers dialogue at 1:20. Lower music by 6dB during dialogue."

**Provide Solutions**
- Don't just identify problems
- Suggest specific fixes
- Reference course materials
- Offer resources

**Encourage Iteration**
- Filmmaking is rewriting
- First drafts are supposed to be rough
- Revision is part of the process
- Growth mindset language

### Written Feedback Template

**Overall Impression**
- What works well
- Main area for improvement
- Overall grade context

**Category-by-Category**
- Specific strengths in each category
- Specific improvements needed
- Concrete suggestions

**Next Steps**
- What to focus on next assignment
- Resources to review
- Skills to practice

**Encouragement**
- Acknowledge effort and growth
- Highlight unique strengths
- Express confidence in improvement

## Special Considerations

### Tokyo Context Adjustments

**Resource Constraints**
- Grade on resourcefulness, not equipment quality
- Credit creative solutions to limitations
- Value problem-solving highly
- Acknowledge Tokyo-specific challenges

**Cultural Sensitivity**
- Reward culturally appropriate choices
- Credit research and understanding
- Value respectful representation
- Acknowledge learning curve

**Location Challenges**
- Consider filming permission difficulties
- Credit backup planning
- Value adaptability
- Acknowledge weather/crowd issues

### Differentiated Assessment

**Beginner Students**
- Emphasize technical competence
- Credit following instructions
- Value safety and process
- Encourage experimentation

**Advanced Students**
- Expect creative risk-taking
- Value artistic vision
- Demand technical excellence
- Encourage innovation

**Group Projects**
- Individual contribution assessment
- Peer evaluation component
- Process documentation required
- Clear role definitions

## Grading Workflows

### Assignment Grading Process

1. **Watch/Review Completely**
   - First viewing: Overall impression
   - Second viewing: Note specific issues
   - Third viewing: Check rubric categories

2. **Apply Rubric**
   - Score each category
   - Note specific examples
   - Calculate weighted total

3. **Write Feedback**
   - Overall comments
   - Category-specific notes
   - Actionable suggestions
   - Encouragement

4. **Record Grade**
   - Enter in gradebook
   - Return to student
   - Offer office hours if needed

### Peer Review Integration

**Peer Feedback Guidelines**
- Use simplified rubric
- Focus on 2-3 categories
- Require specific examples
- Emphasize constructive tone

**Peer Review Benefits**
- Students learn from each other
- Develops critical eye
- Reduces instructor workload
- Builds community

## Voice and Tone in Assessment

**When Writing Feedback**
- **Encouraging**: "Your composition at 0:30 is excellent - the depth you created really draws the eye"
- **Specific**: "At 1:15, the dialogue is 3dB too quiet. Boost it to match the level at 0:45"
- **Growth-focused**: "This shows real improvement in your lighting from the last assignment"
- **Honest but kind**: "The pacing drags in the middle section. Try cutting 15-20 seconds from 1:00-2:00"
- **Solution-oriented**: "Review the continuity editing module and reshoot the missing reverse angle"
- **Empowering**: "You have a strong eye for composition - now let's work on your editing rhythm"

## Integration with Course

### Pre-Assignment
- Provide rubric with assignment
- Explain criteria in class
- Show examples at each level
- Answer questions

### During Work
- Remind students of criteria
- Offer progress checks
- Provide formative feedback
- Adjust timeline if needed

### Post-Submission
- Grade consistently
- Provide detailed feedback
- Offer revision opportunities
- Discuss in one-on-ones

### Reflection
- Students self-assess using rubric
- Compare self-assessment to grade
- Identify growth areas
- Set goals for next assignment
