# Top Three Selections: Text-Film Pairings

**1. Gilgamesh × The Seventh Seal** (Mortality/Existential)
**2. Medea × Downfall** (Compartmentalized Atrocity)
**3. Wife of Bath × Promising Young Woman** (Perspective/Power)

## Why These Three

| Pairing | Visual Distinctiveness | Text-Film Gap | Layer Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gilgamesh/Seventh Seal** | Extreme B&W contrast, iconic compositions | Ancient poem vs. 1957 Bergman | **High**—silence and geometry do heavy lifting |
| **Medea/Downfall** | Fluorescent bunker, rigid blocking | Greek soliloquy vs. withheld face | **High**—text provides what image refuses |
| **Wife of Bath/Promising Young Woman** | Candy colors, camera deception | Medieval prologue vs. modern bar | **High**—perspective shift as argument |

**These three avoid the problem in weaker entries where narration describes what viewers can already see. Instead, each uses text to fill gaps the image cannot show and narration to reframe what the image seems to show.**

## Revised Treatments

### 1. GILGAMESH × THE SEVENTH SEAL

**The Core Problem with Original**: Narration describes the chess game's symmetry while viewers watch it—redundant. Text overlays repeat phrases from the passage without visual keying.
**The Fix**: Use silence as the third layer. Don't narrate the chess game. Let the B&W image work alone. Save all three layers for the gap between text and image.

**Structure (60 seconds)**:

| Time | Layer | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0:00-0:08** | Text only (no narration) | "Who, O my friend, is unconquered by death?" — appears word by word, stone-texture font, no clip yet |
| **0:08-0:12** | Silence + clip | Chess game begins. No narration. No text. Just Fischer's composition: two figures, flat horizon |
| **0:12-0:18** | Narration enters | "He is not asking. He is building his case." |
| **0:18-0:22** | Text overlays clip | "all we accomplish is wind" — appears over the sea horizon, then dissolves into wind moving the grass |
| **0:22-0:35** | Clip continues | Dance of Death silhouettes. No narration. Text: "If I fall, I shall establish my fame" — keyed to appear behind the dancing figures, as if written on the sky |
| **0:35-0:45** | Narration + text | "Block's fame is invisible. A family escapes. They never learn his name." Text: "fame" appears, then struck through as clip shows Jof seeing the dancers from distance |
| **0:45-0:60** | Final text | "That is what fame looks like stripped of the heroic apparatus" — over black, no clip, voice only |

**Key Innovation**: Text doesn't just quote—it interacts with the image. "Wind" literally becomes wind. "Fame" is erased. The three layers (narration explaining the gap, text embodying the concept, clip showing the visual) each do work the others cannot.

### 2. MEDEA × DOWNFALL

**The Core Problem with Original**: Strong concept but narration over-explains what the image withholds. The power is Hirschbiegel refusing the close-up—narration shouldn't compensate but amplify that refusal.

**Structure (75 seconds)**:

| Time | Layer | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0:00-0:10** | Narration + black | "She does not lose control. She gains it." (No image yet—builds anticipation) |
| **0:10-0:15** | Clip + text | Magda brushing hair. Text appears in nurse's log font: "Arm yourself for this, my heart" — as if she's writing it |
| **0:15-0:18** | Sound design only | Cyanide capsule clicks. No narration. No text. The sound is the argument |
| **0:18-0:30** | Clip: The Hallway | Camera follows Magda's back. No text. No narration for 6 seconds. Let the absence land |
| **0:30-0:38** | Text keyed to her back | "forget they are your children" — appears on her white coat, moving with her fabric as she walks |
| **0:38-0:50** | Narration returns | "Euripides wrote what Hirschbiegel correctly will not fabricate." Text: "mourn them later" — appears, then fades to "later" alone as she turns the corner |
| **0:50-1:05** | Clip: Goebbels upstairs | No text. Narration: "For her, there is no later. She knows it. The 'later' is a lie she tells herself to get through the act." |
| **1:05-1:15** | Final text over black | "Syndesis is alignment. The content of the alignment is everything." |

**Key Innovation**: Text is diegetic-adjacent—appearing as if written by the character or system (nurse's log, coat fabric). The 6-second silence in the hallway forces viewers to feel what the film withholds. Narration doesn't describe the image; it contextualizes the absence.

### 3. WIFE OF BATH × PROMISING YOUNG WOMAN

**The Core Problem with Original**: The camera-angle shift is the entire argument, but original narration describes it happening instead of building toward it as revelation.

**Structure (55 seconds)**:

| Time | Layer | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **0:00-0:08** | Text + pop music | "Experience, though no authority ruled in this world, would be enough for me" — in cursive neon, over black, with bar ambience |
| **0:08-0:15** | Clip: The deception | Cassie slumped. Camera angled down. No narration. Text: "Who wrote the histories, tell me who?" — appears in men's restroom graffiti font, bottom corner |
| **0:15-0:18** | Hard cut + silence | Camera drops to eye level. Cassie vertical. One second of pure silence |
| **0:18-0:28** | Narration enters | "The camera was lying to you. It showed you what the man saw." Text: "if women had written the stories" — appears, then glitches to "if women had written" — the rest erases |
| **0:28-0:40** | Clip continues | Cassie clear-eyed. Text overlays in her handwriting (from the ledger): tally marks appearing one by one |
| **0:40-0:55** | Final synthesis | Dean's office scene. Split screen: Cassie still / Dean fidgeting. Text: "Five" — appears, then multiplies to "Five. Five. Five. Five. Five." — Alison's marriages as data points |

**Key Innovation**: Font and placement carry argument. Graffiti font for the male perspective; glitching text for institutional erasure; her handwriting for reclaimed authority. The hard cut to silence at the perspective shift makes it felt, not explained.

## Technical Specifications for All Three

| Element | Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Text animation** | Never static. Words appear, erase, transform, or move with image motion |
| **Font logic** | Diegetic-adjacent: stone (Gilgamesh), medical log (Medea), neon/graffiti/handwriting (Wife of Bath) |
| **Silence deployment** | 3-6 second stretches where only image works—builds trust, prevents over-narration |
| **Narration timing** | Enters after image establishes, exits before clip resolves |
| **Clip selection** | Shorter than instinct suggests—15-20 second maximum per segment, cut for rhythm not coverage |
