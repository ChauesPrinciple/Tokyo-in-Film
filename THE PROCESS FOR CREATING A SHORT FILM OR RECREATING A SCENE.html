SECTION 2. CREATE A LOGLINE THAT FITS THOSE LIMITS

A logline condenses the essential action of the moment into a single, clear sentence. It identifies who the character is in this beat, what they are trying to do, and what stands in their way. Like a thematic statement or a clear line of conflict in a screenplay, the logline gives shape to both the explicit action the viewer will see and the implicit meaning suggested by how that action unfolds.

A useful logline for a short moment or recreated scene focuses on what can be filmed: an observable goal, a concrete obstacle, and the tension created when the two meet. It should guide the choices you will make with blocking, framing, and composition. If the moment is too internal or relies on information the camera cannot show, the logline needs to be simplified until the action is readable on screen.
