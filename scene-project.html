The Scene Project

Recreate. Adapt. Create.
Your journey into Tokyo's cinematic soul.

In this course, you will work in self-selected teams of three to create a 2 to 8-minute short film. At the heart of this project is a deeper appreciation of film and its connection to Tokyo.

You will recreate, adapt, or craft a new scene inspired by the films we analyze, using Tokyo as your creative playground. This process isn�t about technical mastery. It�s about embracing the art of experimentation, playing with storytelling techniques, and finding your own voice within the world of cinema. By engaging with these films, you�ll explore how they capture the essence of Tokyo, not just as a backdrop, but as a character within each story.

The Assignment: Filming a Scene

Your group will recreate, adapt, or design a scene inspired by one of the films analyzed in the course. Use Tokyo�s rich variety of locations to enrich your visual storytelling.

1. Recreate a Scene

Faithfully recreate an iconic scene from one of the assigned films, focusing on how you can replicate its cinematography, lighting, and mood.

2. Adapt a Scene

Take inspiration from a film and modify its setting, characters, or tone to create something fresh while staying true to the original's core themes.

3. Create a New Scene

Design a completely new scene inspired by a film�s style or narrative, showcasing your unique interpretation of the film's mood or visual language.

Project Development Stages

I. Pre-Production

  • Group Formation Choose your team (groups of 3) and brainstorm ideas, considering technical challenges and creative possibilities.
  • Greenlight Process Pitch your concept and production plan to an instructor (your producer). Project approval is required to move forward.
  • Deliverables Submit your script, storyboard, and shot list. Create an animatic if applicable to visualize pacing.

II. Production

  • Filming Shoot your scene(s) based on your storyboard. Pay attention to camera composition, lighting, and style.
  • Directing Collaborative direction is key. Manage the set and ensure shots align with your pre-production vision.

III. Post-Production

  • Editing Use color grading, sound design, and VFX. Apply editing styles relevant to your scene (e.g., pastiche).
  • Final Cut Submit the polished version including titles and credits (2-8 mins).

Educational Goals

  • Skill Application Apply cinematography, editing, and storytelling techniques.
  • Creative Interpretation Engage with iconic scenes through your own lens.
  • Collaboration Develop essential teamwork and communication skills.
  • Portfolio Development Produce a finished short film for your professional portfolio.

Evaluation Criteria

Your project is assessed on process, experimentation, and creative exploration, not just technical mastery.

  • Process and Exploration: Did you try new things and embrace creative uncertainty
  • Creative Adaptation: How well did you bring your unique voice to the material
  • Collaboration: How effectively did the team work together and support peers
  • Reflection: Demonstrate growth through the experience.

Final Submission Requirements

Deadline: The final film is due by 11:59 p.m. on May 26th.

What to Submit

  • Final Film: 2 to 8 minutes, 720p MP4 format. Must include titles and credits.
  • Pre-Production Materials: Updated Script, Storyboard, Shot List, Production Schedule.
  • Production Materials: Dailies and B-roll (aim for 10x footage relative to runtime).
  • Self-Evaluation: Due May 28th. Reflect on contribution and lessons learned (Written, Oral, or Video).

Collaborative Requirement

Each student must assist another group�s project for at least two hours (as cast or crew). Include proof (photos, logs) in your submission.

Extra Credit

Behind-the-Scenes Reel: A 720p MP4 video highlighting your production process, funny moments, and outtakes (integrated into credits or separate).

"No Time for Second-Guessing"

This project is designed to let you experiment and explore the possibilities of filmmaking. Dive into the process, try new things, collaborate with your peers, and most importantly, enjoy the exploration. Your final product is a reflection of that adventure.
